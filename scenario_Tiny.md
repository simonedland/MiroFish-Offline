The 15 SMS Agents

A small SMS network of ten people. Most are completely ordinary. Their texts reflect daily routines, habits, relationships, and timing patterns. Some message constantly, others only in bursts. Over time, each develops a distinct rhythm.

1. The Teenager

Seventeen years old. Texts in fast bursts, full of slang, abbreviations, and changing plans. Quiet during school hours, then suddenly active from mid-afternoon onward. Messages revolve around friends, weekend plans, gossip, minor drama, and last-minute coordination.

2. The Office Worker

In their late thirties. Texts mostly during work hours in a polite, clipped tone. Reschedules meetings, sends short updates, apologises for delays, and occasionally complains about work. Activity drops sharply after 6pm.

3. The Parent

Uses SMS almost entirely for logistics. Pickup times, appointments, grocery reminders, school messages, and household coordination. Short, practical texts with a steady undertone of mild stress. Rarely messages for fun.

4. The Elderly Relative

Warm, slow, and formal. Writes in full sentences and checks in on people. Shares small pieces of everyday life — a walk, a TV programme, a visit, the weather. Low message volume, but steady and sincere.

5. The Sports Fan

Mostly quiet until match day. Then suddenly sends a flood of reactions, opinions, complaints about referees, and commentary on results. Messages spike sharply during games and fall off just as quickly afterward.

6. The Shopper

Always waiting for something, returning something, or telling someone about a deal. Sends tracking links, order updates, delivery complaints, and occasional recommendations. Creates a low, constant background hum of everyday consumer life.

7. The Traveler

Usually quiet, except when in transit. Then the messages become frequent: airport updates, hotel check-ins, delays, restaurant questions, arrival times. Travel periods are easy to spot from the sudden spike in activity.

8. The Gamer

Barely active during the day, then highly active in the evening. Messages are about game sessions, patches, clips, reconnecting, or who is online. Casual tone, lots of abbreviations, occasional all-caps excitement.

9. The Health Person

Routine-driven and upbeat. Messages about workouts, step counts, class times, meal prep, recovery, and whether someone is joining the gym session. Fairly consistent rhythm, with activity tending to stop early at night.
